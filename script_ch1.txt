Imagine dedicating ten entire years of your life to reading the most obscure, unpopular web novel ever written. Well, meet our protagonist, Kim Dokja. His name literally translates to 'only child', but ironically, it also means 'sole reader'. 

He is quite literally the only person on earth who actually survived reading this absolute monster of a story, which contains over three thousand chapters. He’s just an exhausted, completely average contract worker, riding the subway home in Seoul next to his perfect, successful coworker, Yoo Sangah. Honestly? This weird web novel is the only thing keeping him from losing his mind in the corporate rat race.

But today is a massive day. After a decade of writing, the author has finally posted the very last chapter! Dokja leaves a heartfelt comment to say thanks. And shockingly, the author actually replies! They thank him for being their sole supporter, gift him a direct copy of the full completed manuscript, and drop a totally casual bombshell: 'The story will become a paid service... starting tomorrow at 7 PM.'

Fast forward to the exact next day. The clock strikes exactly 7:00 PM. Dokja is riding the subway... when suddenly, the train screeches to a violent, metal-bending halt! Sparks fly everywhere. The lights completely shut off. The passengers are absolutely freaking out in the pitch black train car.

But then... out of the darkness... a bizarre, fluffy mythological goblin, known as a Dokkaebi, spawns right in front of them in thin air! It smiles and gleefully announces to the terrified crowd: 'The free service of planetary system 8612 has ended! The main scenario... has started!'

Dokja freezes in pure shock. Wait a second... a sudden blackout? A floating goblin? He realizes instantly... This is the exact opening scene of the novel he just finished! 

The apocalypse has officially begun. And out of 8 billion people? Kim Dokja is the only human alive who knows exactly how this entire nightmare ends.
